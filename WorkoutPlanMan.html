High Intensity Interval Training

High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) involves doing sharp, intense bursts of cardio exercise followed by short rest periods.

HIIT is a fat burning powerhouse; the short intervals will push you harder than you’d be able if doing longer, steady state exercises. This unsustainable, intense burst of energy output revs up the metabolism and increases the amount of calories burned for hours after your workout. This “after burn” process can last up to 36 hours following a HIIT training session. The key is to rev up your metabolism and keep it cranking to turn your body into a fat burning machine. HIIT can be performed on any workout device, or even with just a series of body weight exercises. This program utilizes a variety of both to achieve optimum results.

Disclaimer High intensity interval training is designed to push your heart rate to the max for short bursts of time. We highly recommend consulting your physician before performing this or any intense exercise program. Train at your own risk and always error on the side of safety.

Important If you aren’t currently training regularly and well conditioned, start slow. During the first 2 weeks of starting this workout please only train at 75-80% intensity to get used to the exercises and condition your muscles. If you go too hard at first, you might become too sore to keep schedule or worse yet, cause an injury. Before starting an exercise ensure you are warmed up before to fully activate and prevent injury.

If any exercise feels like it may aggravate something, please stop immediately. You should always go at your own pace and consult your physician before starting this or any exercise program.

Training should be very focused and intense, with perfect form. Sacrifice load or resistance for form and intensity. Ego has no place in this training. We train smart with efficiency, intensity and purpose.

Pro Tip 1

It’s ideal to train first thing in the AM on an empty stomach with a 12 hour fast since your last meal. Water, black coffee, pre-workout are ok, but no calories or sugar. Training fasted will force your body to call upon its fat reserves for better results.

Workout Notes

Perform these circuits by moving through each exercise per round, ideally with high intensity and without rest between exercises. Upon completing each round take a 1-minute rest, then repeat the round from the start. Continue until all rounds are complete. The goal is not to rest between exercises until each round is complete. Exercises are programmed to allow for muscle recovery, even during continuous exercise by alternating the target area. Train each exercise as if it’s your last. Go all out! Focus intensely on exhausting the targeted muscle with perfect form.
